7 Wonders of the World

1. Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal is located in Agra, India.
It was built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan.
The monument was made in memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal.
It is one of the best examples of Mughal architecture.
The building is made of white marble.
It attracts millions of visitors every year.
It is also considered a symbol of love.

2. Great Wall of China

The Great Wall of China is one of the longest structures ever built.
It stretches over 21,000 kilometers.
It was constructed to protect China from invasions.
The wall was built over many dynasties.
It is made of stone, bricks, and wood.
It is visible even from space according to popular belief.
Today, it is a major tourist attraction.

3. Petra (Jordan)

Petra is an ancient city in Jordan.
It is also called the "Rose City" because of its red rocks.
The city was carved into sandstone mountains.
It was built by the Nabataeans.
Petra was an important trading center.
The city remained hidden for centuries.
Now it is one of the most visited archaeological sites.

4. Christ the Redeemer (Brazil)

Christ the Redeemer is a huge statue in Rio de Janeiro.
It stands 98 feet tall.
It is located on Mount Corcovado.
The statue represents Jesus Christ.
It was completed in 1931.
It is made of reinforced concrete and soapstone.
It is one of Brazil’s most iconic landmarks.

5. Machu Picchu (Peru)

Machu Picchu is an ancient Incan city.
It is located high in the Andes Mountains.
It was built in the 15th century.
It is often called the “Lost City of the Incas.”
The city is known for its stone construction.
It was rediscovered by Hiram Bingham in 1911.
It is now a protected UNESCO World Heritage site.

6. Chichen Itza (Mexico)

Chichen Itza is a famous Mayan archaeological site.
It is located in Mexico’s Yucatan Peninsula.
The main pyramid is called El Castillo.
It was a major political and economic center.
The city is known for its advanced architecture.
It also contains temples and observatories.
It is visited by millions of tourists every year.

7. Colosseum (Italy)

The Colosseum is located in Rome, Italy.
It is an ancient amphitheater.
It was built by Emperor Vespasian.
Gladiator fights were held here.
It could hold around 50,000 spectators.
The Colosseum is made of stone and concrete.
It remains one of Rome’s most famous attractions.
